COMPUTER SCIENCE - BS
Texas A&M University

The computer science curriculum is designed to prepare students to enter the rapidly expanding field of computing. The four-year undergraduate curriculum provides sound preparation in computing, science, mathematics, English and statistics.

PROGRAM EDUCATIONAL OBJECTIVES
1. Graduates use computer science principles to identify and solve emerging technological and societal problems ethically.
2. Graduates who enter the workforce become effective leaders and innovators.
3. Graduates who pursue advanced degrees succeed in prestigious graduate programs.
4. Graduates engage in life-long learning to adapt to new technologies.

NOTES
- A grade of C or better is required in all CSCE courses.
- Of the 18 computer science elective hours: 3 must be systems electives, 3 must be software and languages electives, 3 must be information and applications electives, 9 must be upper-level CS electives.
- Of the 21 University Core Curriculum hours: 3 from creative arts, 3 from social and behavioral sciences, 3 from language/philosophy/culture, 6 from American history, 6 from government/political science.
- All students must complete a high-impact experience to graduate.
- This program is approved to be offered at Texas A&M University at Galveston campus.
